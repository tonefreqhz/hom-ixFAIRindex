# Report

## Introduction
- This document provides a comprehensive analysis of the relationship between the money supply, mortgage lending, absorption rates, and the supply of affordable housing.
- Key findings indicate that effective demand for affordable housing must be met through innovative credit mechanisms, distinct from traditional mortgage lending.

## Section 1: Money Supply and Mortgage Lending
- The money supply (M3) is significantly influenced by mortgage lending, with 0.75% of new mortgage lending contributing to M3.
- **Key Data**:
  - Outstanding value of residential mortgage loans: £1,675.8 billion.
  - Total value of mortgaged property (pre-potential fall): £2.9 trillion.
  - Loan-to-value (LTV) ratio: 57.786%.
  - Potential value of mortgaged property (post-35% fall): £1.875 trillion.
- The close ties between the housing market and the overall financial system highlight the need for careful monitoring of mortgage lending practices.

## Section 2: Absorption Rates
- Absorption rates (AR) determine the speed at which new housing stock is taken up by the market.
- A higher AR indicates quicker sales and occupancy, stabilizing or increasing housing prices.
- Conversely, a lower AR may suggest oversupply or mismatched demand, leading to price decreases.

## Section 3: Planning Value Capture (PVC)
- PVC represents 35% of the value created by new developments that can be reinvested into affordable housing projects.
- While it provides significant funding, it is insufficient alone to meet the entire affordable housing need.

## Section 4: Effective Demand and Affordable Housing Need
- Effective demand (HD) must be directed toward those in need of affordable housing.
- A new circuit of credit creation is essential to avoid expanding overall debt levels while meeting housing needs.

## Section 5: New Circuit of Credit Creation (NCC)
- The NCC aims to provide non-inflationary, rent-controlled credit for affordable housing.
- This mechanism operates separately from traditional housing finance, ensuring funds are allocated specifically for affordable housing projects.

## Conclusion
- The analysis underscores the necessity of innovative credit solutions to address affordable housing needs effectively.
- By integrating the limitations of the free market and the financialization of housing, the report emphasizes that the affordable housing crisis is fundamentally financial, necessitating targeted policy interventions.
